Michel Platini, known as "Le Roi" (The King), is the definitive number 10 of the 1980s and arguably the most intelligent passer the game has ever seen. He revolutionized the role of the playmaker by not only creating but finishing at a rate higher than most strikers. Winning three consecutive Ballon d'Ors, he dominated the toughest league in the world (Serie A) while playing for Juventus. His performance at Euro 1984, where he scored 9 goals in 5 games to lead France to the title, stands as the greatest individual performance in a single international tournament in football history.

Analysis

Platini possessed a supreme, almost disdainful confidence. He famously said, "I don't run, I make the ball run." His psychological profile is that of a grandmaster playing chess while others play checkers. He was demanding, vocal, and intellectually superior to his opponents. He did not rely on physical attributes but on processing the game faster than anyone else.

‚ö° Key Matches & Milestones

Jun 23, 1984 | The Perfect Game
France 3-2 Portugal (Euro Semi-Final). In one of the greatest matches ever, Platini scores the winner in the 119th minute. He finishes the tournament with 9 goals, a record for a midfielder that may never be broken.
May 29, 1985 | Tragedy & Triumph
Juventus 1-0 Liverpool (Heysel). On a dark night for football, Platini scores the winning penalty to give Juventus their first European Cup. A bittersweet moment in history.
Dec 8, 1985 | The Lost Masterpiece
Juventus vs Argentinos Juniors (Intercontinental). Platini scores one of the greatest goals ever (chest control, sombrero over defender, volley) which is wrongly disallowed. He responds by lying on the pitch in protest, an iconic image. He later scores the winning penalty.
1982-1985 | The Golden Era
Becomes the first player to win the Ballon d'Or three times in a row, cementing his status as the undisputed king of 80s football alongside Maradona.
