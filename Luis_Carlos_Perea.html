Player Overview

Luis Carlos "Coroncoro" Perea is widely regarded as one of the greatest defenders in Colombian football history. He began his career at Independiente Medellín in 1983 but achieved legendary status at Atlético Nacional. He was a pillar of the "Puros Criollos" team that won the historic 1989 Copa Libertadores, a campaign where the defense kept 12 clean sheets. A tough, disciplined, and imposing center-back, Perea was renowned for his excellent tackling, aerial prowess, and leadership. He was a fixture for the Colombia national team, earning 78 caps and playing in two World Cups (Italia 1990, USA 1994) and four Copa Américas (1987, 1989, 1991, 1993). He also won multiple titles with Nacional, including two Copas Interamericanas and a Copa Merconorte.
