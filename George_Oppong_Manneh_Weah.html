Analysis

Weah's psychological profile is that of a statesman in football boots. He possessed an innate belief in his abilities (demonstrated by his solo goals) combined with a profound sense of responsibility towards his team and his country. He famously funded the Liberian national team out of his own pocket. On the pitch, he was fierce but fair, a player who led by example through sheer work rate and moments of magic.

‚ö° Key Matches & Milestones

Nov 23, 1994 | Munich Masterclass
Bayern Munich 0-1 PSG. Champions League Group Stage. Weah scores a wonder goal, dribbling past multiple defenders before firing into the top corner. A goal that announced his Ballon d'Or candidacy to the world.
Aug 27, 1995 | The Milan Debut
Makes his Serie A debut for AC Milan against Padova. Scores within 6 minutes and assists another. The San Siro falls in love instantly with his power and grace.
Sep 8, 1996 | The Goal of the Century
AC Milan vs Hellas Verona. Weah collects the ball from a corner inside his own penalty box. He runs the entire length of the pitch (90+ meters), evading three defenders, and slots it home. Perhaps the greatest solo goal in Serie A history.
May 12, 2000 | FA Cup Glory
Starts for Chelsea in the last FA Cup final at the old Wembley Stadium vs Aston Villa. Though Di Matteo scores the winner, Weah's loan spell is capped with major silverware in England.
Jan 2002 | The Farewell
Plays for Liberia in the African Cup of Nations, a tournament his funding largely made possible. A fitting international swan song for the man who carried his nation.
