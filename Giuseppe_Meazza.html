üåç Global Impact Analysis

Giuseppe Meazza is arguably the greatest Italian player of all time. He was a phenomenon who combined the goalscoring instinct of a pure number 9 with the playmaking genius of a number 10. Famous for his "foglia morta" (dead leaf) dribbling style and his ability to humiliate goalkeepers by baiting them out of goal, he was the heart of Vittorio Pozzo's Italy that won back-to-back World Cups. His celebrity status in Milan was akin to a movie star, known for sleeping at brothels the night before games and arriving minutes before kick-off, only to score a hat-trick.

Analysis

Meazza was the ultimate "player's player." He hated training and loved the high life, yet his teammates respected him because he always delivered. He possessed an unshakable self-belief. Famously, when a coach tried to make him train harder, he just scored hat-tricks in protest. He was a natural leader on the pitch through talent, though less disciplined off it.
