Player Overview

Faryd Camilo Mondragón Alí's career spanned an extraordinary 24 years at professional level, culminating in his record-breaking World Cup appearance at age 43. Starting at Deportivo Cali, he moved to Argentina before finding his greatest success in Turkey with Galatasaray, where he won the UEFA Cup in 2000. His longest stint came at German club 1. FC Köln (2001-2007), establishing himself as a reliable Bundesliga goalkeeper with over 150 appearances. After spells in Turkey, Argentina, and Colombia, he joined the Colombia squad for the 2014 World Cup as third-choice goalkeeper. When coach José Pékerman substituted him into the final group game against Japan, Mondragón became the oldest player in World Cup history at 43 years and 3 days. His longevity and professionalism made him a respected figure in Colombian football.
